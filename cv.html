While many of these sound cliche, they are real skills I've noticed are effective in my years of solving problems in teams. Thank you to our robotics team Team4Robotics!

Teamwork skills

Nothing better than being a part of a team!

Listening

Listening carefully and asking a lot questions

Communication

Clear and precise communication benefits all. A team member needs to be able to present their thoughts effectively

Team resource management

We're all different. Learning how to take advantage of that is one of the key steps to making a well functioning team

Problem-solving skills

Problem-solving is at the heart of what I do. More complex, more better.

Subdivision

Don't tackle everything at once, it's too complicated. Instead, divide and isolate the smallest problems and solve them individually. That how you pull through in lengthty projects!

Brainstorming and discussion

Relating to teamwork skills, take advantage of the ideas different people. Eventually, choose the solution as a team after a discussion!

Resources

Besides team resources, others like time and money often have an effect on which solutions are best suited. A quick fix may not be ideal, and in the best case not needed (as it was planned for earlier), but in the moment it may be what's needed!

Testing

Solutions must be thoroughly tested, even in unexpected conditions. Proper testing can alleviate many future problems!

Documenation

Often overlooked, documenation is the key to making things run in the future.

Iteration

Once you complete all the steps above, don't stop there! Repeat and repeat, refine the solution. Eventually, the gains will return diminishing results and you need to know when to stop!
